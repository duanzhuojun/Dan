Simulation of Back and Arms II

Objectives: 1. Find the optimal position for lifting a load.   2. Apply the conditions of equilibrium to determine the amount of force that is exerted by the person’s Erector Spinae muscle FES when the person is bending over to support a load.   3. Calculate the magnitude of the Joint Reaction Force JRF.   4. Find out how the force on the person’s erector spinae muscle and the joint reaction force depend on positions.

Instruction:

The simulation is based on a normally proportioned person. You can adjust the gestures of the person by inputting different values of ΘTNH and ΘAH. For example, ΘTNH=0˚ and ΘAH=90˚ when a person stands with arms leveled.

When you try to find the optimal position for lifting a load to avoid potential injury, you aim to find low values on erector spinae muscle and the joint reaction force. (Hint: in order to achieve the goal, please control variables by keeping weight and height of the person, weight of the load fixed and changing ΘTNH and ΘAH values.)
